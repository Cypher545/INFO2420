Website Critiques

https://www.jamilin.com/

Questions:

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Answers:

  1. My first impression of jamilin.com, was that I was about to get scammed out of all my hard earned cash. It is extremely disorganized and messy; there are links everywhere, covering up blocks of text, and everything is plastered with her face and name.
  2. There doesn't really seem to be much in the way of a flow of information. It does have a bar along the top with links to different pages, but these are also linked immediately below the bar, with more links on top of them. It appears to be based on columns and information is organized in blocks, but there is no real structure to it.
  3. The site structure is confusing, but somehow it also makes sense. The idea of the site is to sell you Jami's work, and immediately tells you that they take all kinds of credit cards and directs you to her various scams with all of the myriad links.
  4. The graphics and layout definitely detract from the site. It feels extremely unprofessional and makes it feel as if this woman is attempting to be some kind of cult leader.
  5. To Jami's credit, the homepage loaded almost instantly, even though I'm sitting behind a VPN.
  6. The text and the color is readble, but the text is a bit small in some places, and along the top navigation bar, the white text is a bit hard to read against a couple of the different colors (the yellow and light blue for example.)
  7. The site did have a navigation bar along the top, although it is just links to different websites that Jami owns, with one of the links even redirecting to the same page. The navigation bar could probably be improved a lot by being simplified and made more clear.
  8. There isn't really a clear color scheme, other than "rainbow", and there doesn't really seem to be much consistency on the different pages.
  9. The site actually does respond to different window sizes, although it was clearly designed for wider screens. On smaller screens, the floating links cover up the text in some places.
  10. Everything about this site is unusual. I was, however, offered free super powers, which is kind of cool.
